Legal Name Change Process – Step by Step
1.	Name Change Affidavit
•	Prepare an affidavit on stamp paper mentioning your old name, new name, full address, and reason for change.
•	Get it signed by you (and witnesses if required) and notarized by a notary public.[5][6]
2.	Newspaper Advertisement
•	Publish your name change advertisement in two newspapers: one English and one regional language.
•	This acts as public notice and proof for Gazette, passport, and other authorities.[6][3]
3.	Gazette Notification
•	Submit your affidavit, newspaper clippings, and application to the Department of Publication (Central Gazette) or Maharashtra Gazette, along with the prescribed fee (offline or online as applicable).
•	After verification, your name change is printed in the official Gazette.[1][3]
4.	Receive Approved Gazette Copy
•	Once approved, you receive the Gazette notification (printed or downloadable PDF).
•	This becomes your main legal document for updating PAN, Aadhaar, passport, bank, and other records.[3][5]

•	Affidavit preparation: Make and notarize a clear affidavit with your old name, new name, address, reason for change, and a statement that it is not for any illegal purpose.
