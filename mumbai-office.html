Step by Step Guide

Legal Name Change Process

01

Name Change Affidavit

Prepare an affidavit on stamp paper mentioning your old name, new name, full address, and reason for change. Get it signed by you (and witnesses if required) and notarized by a notary public.

02

Newspaper Advertisement

Publish your name change advertisement in two newspapers: one English and one regional language. This acts as public notice and proof for Gazette, passport, and other authorities.

03

Gazette Notification

Submit your affidavit, newspaper clippings, and application to the Department of Publication (Central Gazette) or Maharashtra Gazette, along with the prescribed fee (offline or online as applicable). After verification, your name change is printed.

04

Receive Approved Gazette Copy

Once approved, you receive the Gazette notification (printed or downloadable PDF). This becomes your main legal document for updating PAN, Aadhaar, passport, bank, and other records.

Gazette Publication – A Simple Guide

Affidavit Preparation

Make and notarize a clear affidavit with your old name, new name, address, reason for change, and a statement that it is not for any illegal purpose.

Newspaper Advertisement

Publish name change ads in one English and one local language newspaper, as required in most cases.

Gazette Publication

Submit your affidavit, ads, form, and fee to the Central Gazette (egazette.nic.in) or Maharashtra Gazette, depending on your requirement.

Maharashtra Gazette Name Change – Simple Explanation

In Maharashtra, state-level name changes are recorded in the Maharashtra Gazette.

  • Fill the name change form and collect documents.
  • Submit the application online through the official portal or at the notified office.
  • After approval, your name is printed in the Maharashtra Gazette, and you can download or collect the copy.
This proof is used to update Aadhaar, bank accounts, property records, and other documents.

Cost of Gazette in Mumbai (Approximate)

Gazette publication fee usually falls between ₹1,000 and ₹2,000. You may also incur costs for: stamp paper, affidavit drafting, notarisation, newspaper ads, and consultant/service fees.

How to Apply for Gazette Certificate Online in Maharashtra

  • Visit the official Maharashtra portal (for example: dgps.maharashtra.gov.in or through Aaple Sarkar service).
  • Go to “Change in Name” section.
  • Create an account or log in, read the instructions, and download the relevant form.
  • Fill, sign, scan the form and upload with other documents.
  • Pay the fee online and submit the application.

Procedures & Checklists

Important Name Change Procedures & Checklists

Gazette for Aadhaar Name Change

First complete your legal name change (affidavit, newspaper ads, Gazette notification). Visit the nearest Aadhaar Seva Kendra. Carry: Gazette copy, ID proof with old name and photo, and any additional document based on reason (marriage certificate, etc.). After Aadhaar is updated, use the same Gazette notification to update other documents.

Is Gazette Enough for Passport Name Change?

In most cases, you need a Notarized affidavit for name change, the Gazette notification as legal proof, and the Newspaper advertisement as public notice. With these, you can apply for re-issue or update at Passport Seva Kendra or Regional Passport Office.

Cost to Change Your Name in India (Rough Idea)

Typical total cost: around ₹3,000–₹5,000, including: Affidavit and stamp paper, Notary charges, Two newspaper ads, Gazette publication fee, and any consultant/service fees, if used.

Documents Needed for Gazette Name Change

  • Application letter to the concerned officer.
  • Notarized affidavit.
  • Two signed copies of the name change notice.
  • Original newspaper page with your name change ad.
  • ID proof (Aadhaar, voter ID, PAN, etc.).
  • For minors: birth certificate/Aadhaar and parent or guardian application.
  • For government employees: “Deed Changing Name/Surname” form and employer letter.

Downloading Maharashtra Gazette Name Change Certificate

Visit egazette.nic.in. Click “Search Gazette” and choose “Weekly/Extraordinary Gazette” and Part (often Part IV). Find the issue where your name appears and download the PDF.

Charni Road & Mumbai Area – Practical Information

There is no Central Gazette application office in Mumbai. Consultants near Charni Road, Marine Lines, and CST help with services like Affidavit drafting, ad booking, and submitting applications to Delhi on your behalf.

Using Consultants Near Charni Road

Typically, you share details, they draft and notarize the affidavit, book newspaper ads, prepare all forms, send the file to New Delhi, and share the final PDF with you.

Do You Need to Visit Charni Road?

No, visiting is not compulsory. Many consultants offer complete online service via phone, email, and WhatsApp, which is especially useful for NRIs.
