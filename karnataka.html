What is a Gazette Name Change?

A public record that establishes your new name's validity.

This publication serves as primary proof linking your old and new names, required for updating official documents like Aadhaar, PAN, passport, and bank records. It ensures transparency to prevent fraud and is mandated under acts like the General Clauses Act, 1897.

Legal Purpose

Mandatory proof for Govt IDs & Exams.

Validity

Standalone legal standing under Evidence Act.

How It Works Step-by-Step

Once you send your documents, we'll kick off your name change process right away. We handle everything from Affidavit to Publication.

1

Affidavit Creation

We draft a clear, notarized affidavit spelling out your old name, new name, and reason—tailored just for you.

2

Newspaper Ad

Your name change gets announced publicly in a trusted national or local Karnataka paper (English + Kannada).

3

Digital Prep (CD)

We format the exact Gazette notice in MS Word and burn it to CD for seamless submission as required by the office.

4

Govt Fee Payment

We handle the challan payment (around ₹1,100+), attach the receipt, and keep everything transparent.

Application Filing

We bundle affidavit, ads, CD, proofs, fees and submit it to the Gazette office via post, courier, or eRajyapatra portal.

6

Publication

Once approved (4-6 weeks typical), your notice hits the Gazette of India (Part-IV) or Karnataka eRajyapatra.

Documents Required

Documents Required for Gazette Name Change in Karnataka include these essentials for eRajyapatra submission.

Category Required Documents
Standard
  • Completed Gazette application form
  • Notarised affidavit on stamp paper
  • ID Proof (Aadhaar, PAN, Passport)
  • Address Proof (Aadhaar, Utility Bill)
  • Passport-size photographs (2-3)
Additional
  • Newspaper ad copies (English + Kannada)
  • Marriage certificate / Divorce order (if applicable)
  • Adoption papers (for adopted children)
  • Parent/Guardian ID (for minors)

Who Can Apply?

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Mismatch between affidavit and newspaper ads (e.g., spellings).
  • Spelling errors in names across documents.
  • Blurry, incomplete, or wrong-format document scans.
  • Signature differences between affidavit, forms, and IDs.

Prevention Tip: Align everything to the affidavit first!

After Gazette Publication

Once published, use the PDF to update documents in this order:

  1. Aadhaar: Submit Gazette copy online.
  2. PAN: Apply via NSDL/UTI portal.
  3. Bank/Salary: Visit branches with Gazette.
  4. Passport: Reissue via Passport Seva.

Related Guides & FAQs

For minors, the process involves an affidavit signed by parents/guardians, followed by newspaper ads. The Gazette application is filed by the parent on behalf of the child.
Apply for a correction on the NSDL or UTIITSL website. You will need to upload the Gazette Notification copy along with your Aadhaar (with the new name) as proof.
You must apply for a "Re-issue" of passport under the category "Change in Personal Particulars". The Gazette notification is a primary document accepted for this.
